Registered Residential Carpenter (RRC) proposal

Registered Residential Carpenter (RRC)

A renovation-focused middle-tier licensing pathway for highly experienced carpenters working in existing-home residential renovation.

The RRC proposal is designed to improve regulatory fit, protect builder scope, recognise real renovation expertise, and support housing opportunity by creating a clearer pathway for defined residential renovation work in Victoria.

Core position

This is not about replacing builders or lowering standards. It is about creating a practical renovation-focused pathway that better matches the real structure of work already happening across Victoria.

What is the RRC?

The Registered Residential Carpenter is a proposed middle-tier licence for highly experienced carpenters specialising in existing-home residential renovation. It is designed to recognise capability in renovation-specific work that sits between the standard trade pathway and full builder registration.

The goal is a clearer legal and regulatory fit for practical renovation work — while keeping registered builders responsible for new homes, major structural construction, major extensions, multi-storey buildings, and large developments.

10+ years Intended experience level for a serious renovation-focused pathway
Existing homes The core focus of the proposal is work in real lived-in residential properties
Clear boundaries Builder scope remains protected for larger and more structural work
Why the RRC exists

Renovation work is now a major part of Victoria’s housing activity, yet the licensing framework does not clearly reflect the specialist renovation role many experienced carpenters already perform in practice.

  • Recognises highly experienced renovation carpenters
  • Improves regulatory fit for existing-home renovation
  • Supports housing opportunity and renovation productivity
  • Protects builder scope for major structural work
What the RRC would recognise
  • Deep renovation experience in existing homes
  • Capability to manage defined non-structural renovation projects
  • Understanding of compliance, sequencing, and trade coordination
  • Real on-site responsibility without undermining builder authority

Scope and boundaries

This proposal works only if the boundaries are clear. The RRC is for renovation-focused work in existing homes. It does not replace the builder pathway.

RRC scope examples
  • Bathroom renovations
  • Kitchen renovations
  • Decks, pergolas, and verandas
  • Internal renovations
  • Window and door upgrades
  • Non-structural residential renovation work
Builder scope remains protected
  • New homes
  • Major structural construction
  • Major extensions
  • Multi-storey buildings
  • Large developments

Future structural endorsement

A future advanced endorsement could allow limited structural renovation work in existing homes under strict limits, clear safeguards, and permit-linked boundaries.

Possible future scope

  • Defined small load-bearing wall changes
  • Limited structural stud wall work
  • Existing-home structural alterations within set limits

What would still stay out

  • New-home framing
  • Major structural construction
  • Open-ended structural authority

Why it matters

It creates a future pathway for highly experienced renovation carpenters without blurring the line between specialist renovation work and builder-level authority.

Why this supports housing opportunity

Victoria’s housing system is not only about new homes. It is also about renovating, adapting, upgrading, and extending existing housing stock. A better fit between licensing and real renovation work can support that capacity.

Better regulatory fit

Aligns the licensing structure more closely with the work actually being carried out in the field.

Stronger renovation productivity

Gives defined renovation work a more practical pathway while preserving standards and accountability.

Better use of existing skills

Recognises experienced carpenters already delivering real value in the renovation sector.

Important principle

The purpose is not to weaken protections. The purpose is to create a smarter, clearer, more practical framework that recognises specialist renovation capability while maintaining strong boundaries.

Frequently asked questions

Does the RRC replace builders?

No. Builder scope remains fully protected for new homes, major structural construction, major extensions, multi-storey buildings, and large developments.

Why focus on existing-home renovation?

Renovation is a specialist skill set requiring judgment, sequencing, rectification, and practical problem-solving inside real houses.

Would RRC holders do structural work?

The core RRC concept is renovation-focused and non-structural. A future endorsement may allow limited structural work under strict rules and defined boundaries.

Who is behind this initiative?

The proposal is led by Daniel Sonsie through Carpenters for Housing Reform Victoria as an industry-led reform initiative.
