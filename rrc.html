A practical, renovation‑focused licensing pathway for highly experienced Victorian carpenters.

Why the RRC Exists

A licensing pathway designed for existing‑home renovation.

The Registered Residential Carpenter (RRC) is a proposed middle‑tier licence that recognises highly experienced carpenters who specialise in residential renovation work. It improves regulatory fit by aligning licensing requirements with the real‑world work performed by renovation carpenters.

The RRC strengthens consumer protection, supports renovation capacity, and provides a clear, practical pathway for experienced tradespeople without replacing or undermining registered builders.

What the RRC Recognises

Competency in residential renovation carpentry.

The RRC recognises the skills, experience, and responsibility required to deliver high‑quality renovation outcomes in existing homes. It is designed for carpenters who routinely manage small‑to‑medium renovation projects that do not involve major structural works.

RRC Scope of Work

Renovation‑focused scope that protects builder responsibilities.

The RRC licence would allow experienced carpenters to contract and manage non‑structural and light‑structural renovation work in existing homes, including:

  • Bathroom renovations
  • Kitchen renovations
  • Decks
  • Pergolas
  • Verandas
  • Internal renovations
  • Window and door upgrades
  • General non‑structural residential renovation work

This scope is intentionally limited to protect the responsibilities and domain of registered builders.

Builder Scope Protection

Clear boundaries that maintain builder authority.

The RRC does not replace builders. Builder scope remains exclusively required for:

  • New homes
  • Major structural construction
  • Major extensions
  • Multi‑storey buildings
  • Large developments

The RRC strengthens the system by ensuring renovation carpentry is regulated appropriately while preserving the essential role of registered builders.

Future Structural Endorsement

A possible future RRC‑S endorsement under strict limits.

A future optional endorsement (RRC‑S) could allow limited structural work under defined conditions, subject to strict competency requirements and regulatory safeguards. This would support renovation productivity while maintaining strong consumer protection.

FAQ

Common Questions

Does the RRC replace builders?
No. The RRC is a renovation‑focused licence. Builder scope remains protected and unchanged.

Why is this needed?
Renovation carpentry is a major part of Victoria’s housing ecosystem, yet licensing does not currently reflect the real‑world work performed by experienced carpenters.

Who is the RRC for?
Highly experienced carpenters working primarily in existing‑home renovation.

Will consumers be protected?
Yes. The RRC strengthens consumer protection by ensuring renovation work is performed by appropriately licensed practitioners.
