Registered Residential Carpenter (RRC)

A practical, regulated middle-tier licence pathway for highly experienced carpenters managing defined residential renovation work in Victoria — while protecting builder scope, maintaining standards, and improving housing delivery.

Overview

The Registered Residential Carpenter (RRC) proposal is designed to recognise highly experienced carpenters already delivering real residential renovation work in practice.

It creates a clearer and safer legal framework for renovation-focused work in existing homes, without weakening standards and without replacing the registered builder pathway.

The Problem

$10k threshold

Victoria’s current framework generally pushes domestic building work over $10,000 into the builder pathway.

$20k–$40k bathrooms

Modern bathroom renovations commonly exceed the threshold even when they are standard renovation jobs in today’s market.

$25k–$70k kitchens

Kitchen renovations now regularly sit well above the threshold too, creating a mismatch between the law and practical reality on site.

Experienced carpenters are already solving renovation problems, sequencing trades, managing practical site issues, and delivering defined work in existing homes — but the system does not clearly recognise that real-world role.

The Solution

The answer is not to weaken standards. The answer is to create a practical, regulated pathway that better reflects how renovation work already operates in Victoria.

Recognise Experience

Create a pathway for highly experienced carpenters with real renovation delivery history.

Protect Builder Scope

Keep new homes, major structural builds, and major extensions in the registered builder pathway.

Lift Accountability

Bring more renovation work into a defined, lawful, standards-first structure.

Indicative Scope of Work

RRC Could Cover

  • Bathrooms
  • Kitchens
  • Decks, pergolas and verandahs
  • Internal renovations
  • Rectification and upgrade work
  • Defined renovation work in existing homes

Builder Scope Remains Protected

  • New homes
  • Major structural construction
  • Large extensions
  • Multi-storey residential builds
  • Large domestic building projects

Future Endorsement Pathway

A future structural renovation endorsement could allow limited structural work in existing homes only, subject to strict compliance, permit, and scope controls.

Indicative Eligibility

  • Minimum 10+ years of relevant industry experience
  • Strong record in residential renovation work
  • Understanding of permits, sequencing and compliance
  • Ability to coordinate licensed trades where required
  • Clear practical capability in existing homes

Consumer Protection

Standards First

Any RRC pathway should strengthen accountability, not reduce it.

Clear Scope Limits

Consumers should know exactly what work sits inside the RRC class and what does not.

Proper Documentation

Written contracts, defined responsibilities, and compliance expectations should be mandatory.

Support the Proposal
