Registered Residential Carpenter (RRC) Proposal • Victoria

A practical middle-tier licence pathway for highly experienced renovation carpenters.

The Registered Residential Carpenter proposal is designed to recognise real renovation capability, improve regulatory fit, protect builder scope, and create a clearer legal pathway for experienced carpenters already managing defined residential renovation work in existing homes.

Why the RRC exists

Victoria’s current framework does not clearly recognise the specialist renovation carpenter.

Across Victoria, experienced carpenters are already coordinating renovation delivery, solving existing-house problems, sequencing trades, handling compliance realities, and taking real responsibility on site. The issue is not capability. The issue is that the system does not clearly recognise that specialist renovation role. The RRC proposal is intended to fix that mismatch without weakening standards.

Core RRC model

What the Registered Residential Carpenter pathway would recognise.

RRC could recognise
  • Highly experienced carpenters specialising in residential renovation work
  • Practical project delivery in existing homes
  • Trade sequencing and coordination of licensed trades where required
  • Compliance awareness, permit understanding, and renovation problem-solving
  • Real on-site responsibility within a clearly defined regulated scope
Proposed minimum characteristics
  • Minimum 10+ years relevant experience
  • Demonstrated history in renovation work
  • Defined residential scope only
  • Existing homes rather than new-home authority
  • Clear boundaries that preserve builder responsibility for larger and more structural work
Clear division of responsibility

This proposal is not about replacing builders. It is about matching the right licence to the right work.

Builders remain responsible for
  • All new homes
  • Major structural construction and major framing
  • Major extensions
  • Multi-storey buildings
  • Large residential developments
RRC holders could manage
  • Bathroom renovations
  • Kitchen renovations
  • Window and door upgrades
  • Decks, pergolas, and verandas
  • Internal renovations and rectification work
  • Defined non-structural renovation-focused residential work
Future pathway

A future structural renovation endorsement could exist within strict limits.

Potential future endorsement
  • Defined small load-bearing wall changes
  • Limited structural stud wall work
  • Existing-home structural alterations within set limits
  • Permit-linked, standards-based scope only
What it would not cover
  • New-home framing
  • Major structural construction
  • Open-ended structural authority
  • Large developments or unrestricted builder-level work
Why this makes sense

Renovation work in existing houses is a specialist skill set in its own right.

Existing homes require judgment, rectification, sequencing, tie-in work, and practical structural understanding that often sits most strongly with highly experienced renovation carpenters. That real capability should be recognised inside a safer legal framework instead of being ignored by a builder-only binary.

This is about practical regulatory modernisation: better fit, better recognition, clearer accountability, and stronger alignment between licensing rules and what already happens in the field.

Reform position

Recognise experienced renovation carpenters properly while keeping builder scope, permits, standards, and accountability intact.

That is the heart of the RRC proposal.

Common questions

FAQ

Does the RRC proposal weaken standards?

No. The proposal argues for a clearly defined regulated pathway with boundaries, accountability, and preserved builder scope. The aim is better fit, not lower standards.

Is this meant to replace registered builders?

No. Builders remain responsible for new homes, major structural work, major extensions, and larger developments. The RRC is about renovation-specific recognition, not builder replacement.

Who is this pathway meant for?

Highly experienced carpenters already delivering defined residential renovation work in practice, especially in existing homes where renovation judgment and sequencing matter.
