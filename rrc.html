Registered Residential Carpenter (RRC)

A practical middle-tier licence pathway for highly experienced carpenters managing defined residential renovation work in Victoria.

Overview

The Registered Residential Carpenter (RRC) proposal is designed to recognise highly experienced renovation carpenters already delivering defined residential work in practice, while protecting builder scope and maintaining standards.

Why Reform Is Needed

Victoria’s residential licensing framework no longer reflects the economic and practical reality of modern renovations. High-value kitchen, bathroom, and internal renovation work is now common, yet the system does not clearly recognise the specialist renovation role already being performed on site.

What the RRC Licence Would Do

The RRC would create a regulated, renovation-focused pathway for experienced carpenters with strong practical capability, compliance understanding, and real project delivery experience in existing homes.

Clear Scope of Work

RRC holders could manage defined renovation work such as bathrooms, kitchens, decks, pergolas, verandas, internal renovations, rectification work, and other non-structural residential projects within a clearly regulated scope.

Builders would remain responsible for new homes, major structural construction, major extensions, multi-storey buildings, and large residential developments.

Future Structural Renovation Endorsement

A future advanced endorsement could allow limited structural renovation work in existing homes only, within strict permit, compliance, and scope limits.

Indicative Eligibility

  • Minimum 10+ years experience
  • Demonstrated renovation project experience
  • Understanding of permits, sequencing, and compliance
  • Ability to coordinate licensed trades where required

Consumer Protection

This proposal is about clearer accountability, protected builder scope, and a safer legal framework that better matches the real work being carried out in existing homes.
