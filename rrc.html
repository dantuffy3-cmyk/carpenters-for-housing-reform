Registered Residential Carpenter (RRC)

A renovation‑focused licensing pathway for highly experienced carpenters working in existing‑home residential renovation — improving regulatory fit, protecting builder scope, and supporting renovation capacity across Victoria.

What is the RRC?

A renovation‑focused licensing pathway

The Registered Residential Carpenter (RRC) is a proposed middle‑tier licence for carpenters who specialise in existing‑home renovation. It recognises real‑world capability in bathrooms, kitchens, decks, pergolas, verandas, internal renovations, and other non‑structural residential renovation work.

The RRC does not replace builders. Builder scope remains protected for new homes, major structural construction, major extensions, multi‑storey buildings, and large developments.

Why the RRC exists

Renovation work is a major part of Victoria’s housing system. Many carpenters specialise in this space, yet the current licensing framework does not reflect the practical realities of renovation‑focused work.

  • Recognises highly experienced renovation carpenters
  • Improves regulatory fit for existing‑home renovation
  • Supports housing opportunity and renovation productivity
  • Protects builder scope for major structural work
What the RRC recognises
  • Deep renovation experience
  • Capability to manage non‑structural renovation projects
  • Understanding of compliance, sequencing, and trade coordination
  • Work that does not replace or undermine builders
Scope and boundaries

RRC scope vs builder scope

RRC scope examples
  • Bathroom renovations
  • Kitchen renovations
  • Decks, pergolas, verandas
  • Internal renovations
  • Window and door upgrades
  • Non‑structural residential renovation work
Builder scope remains protected
  • New homes
  • Major structural construction
  • Major extensions
  • Multi‑storey buildings
  • Large developments
Future direction

Future structural endorsement

A future endorsement could allow limited structural renovation work in existing homes under strict limits, clear safeguards, and permit‑linked boundaries — without blurring the line between renovation carpenters and builders.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Does the RRC replace builders?

No. Builder scope remains fully protected for major structural and new‑home work.

Why focus on existing‑home renovation?

Renovation is a specialist skill set requiring judgment, sequencing, and practical problem‑solving.

Will RRCs be allowed to do structural work?

The core RRC is non‑structural. A future endorsement may allow limited structural work under strict rules.

Who is behind this initiative?

The proposal is led by Daniel Sonsie, a Victorian carpenter advocating practical renovation‑focused reform.
