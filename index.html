Campaign priorities

The reform message is structured around three clear priorities.

Recognise real renovation practice

Experienced carpenters already deliver substantial renovation work in existing homes. The regulatory framework should better reflect that practical reality.

Keep the pathway tightly bounded

The proposal is for a defined category of work, supported by explicit limits, exclusions, competency settings, and compliance controls.

Protect builder-led responsibility

New homes, major structural work, broader project control, and wider compliance responsibility remain with domestic builders.

The current problem

Victoria’s renovation settings do not cleanly match modern project costs.

Ordinary renovation projects such as bathrooms, kitchens, decks, internal reconfigurations, and window or door replacements can exceed the current threshold very quickly, even where the work remains contained in scope and carpentry-led in delivery.

That creates a mismatch between the law’s pricing assumptions and the practical shape of renovation work in existing homes.

Practical market reality

Existing-home renovation is a significant share of housing activity and requires a better regulatory fit.

Consumer protection matters

The proposal is about better alignment, not weaker standards or a backdoor builder licence.

Capacity matters too

A clearer pathway can support workforce recognition, industry clarity, and more responsive renovation delivery.

Frequently asked

Quick answers for first-time visitors.

Does this proposal replace domestic builders?

No. The reform is explicitly bounded and keeps builder scope intact for new homes, major structural work, broader project responsibility, and other builder-led functions.

Who is this website for?

It is for policymakers, regulators, builders, carpenters, trainers, housing stakeholders, advisers, and homeowners who want a clear overview before engaging with the full proposal.

What is the best next step if I want more detail?

Open the full RRC proposal page, download the policy brief, and request a 20-minute briefing for a more detailed discussion.
