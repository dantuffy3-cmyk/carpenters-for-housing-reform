The Proposal

The current domestic building threshold no longer reflects the cost of ordinary renovation work. The proposed Registered Residential Carpenter pathway would recognise experienced carpenters already coordinating renovation projects, while preserving the essential role of registered builders in new homes, structural work, and major construction.

Why Reform Is Needed

Victoria’s current domestic building regulations generally require a registered builder to contract residential building work valued above approximately $10,000. While intended to protect consumers, this threshold no longer reflects the reality of modern renovation costs.

In today’s market, many common renovation projects — including bathrooms, kitchens, decks, and internal upgrades — regularly exceed $10,000. As a result, even smaller renovation projects must often be structured through a registered builder, despite experienced carpenters already coordinating much of the day-to-day work on site.

Current Industry Reality

Across Victoria, experienced carpenters already manage day-to-day renovation works, sequence trades, oversee site progress, and deliver outcomes for homeowners. The issue is not whether this work happens. The issue is that the licensing framework does not clearly recognise it.

Proposed Division of Responsibility

Clear boundary

This pathway is renovation-focused. It is not intended to cover new-home framing or new-home construction.

Builders remain responsible for

  • All new homes
  • Structural construction
  • Major extensions
  • Multi-storey buildings
  • Large residential developments

RRC could manage

  • Bathroom renovations
  • Kitchen renovations
  • Window and door upgrades
  • Decks and pergolas
  • Internal renovations
  • Repair and rectification work

Safeguards

  • Defined scope limits
  • Experience requirements
  • Licensed plumbers/electricians unchanged
  • Approvals where required
  • Consumer protection maintained

Experience Requirements

Any Registered Residential Carpenter pathway should apply only to highly experienced tradespeople with significant on-site knowledge of residential construction and renovation work.

  • Minimum 10+ years experience in the carpentry trade
  • Completion of a carpentry apprenticeship
  • Demonstrated experience managing renovation projects
  • Understanding of building compliance and permit requirements
  • Coordination of licensed trades such as plumbing and electrical

Benefits for Victoria

Recognising experienced carpenters through a renovation-focused pathway could create practical benefits for the building industry, homeowners, and the broader Victorian economy.

  • Helping address trade shortages. Allowing experienced carpenters to take responsibility for defined renovation work could increase the number of qualified professionals able to deliver smaller residential projects.
  • Supporting builders. Builders remain responsible for new homes, structural construction, and larger projects, while experienced carpenters could assist with growing renovation demand.
  • Improving housing delivery. Smaller renovation projects may be completed more efficiently, reducing bottlenecks and helping homeowners access qualified trades.
  • Supporting the Victorian economy. More renovation work delivered by qualified tradespeople means more legitimate contracts and greater formal economic activity.

Safeguards & Common Questions

Will this reduce building standards?

No. It strengthens accountability by recognising renovation-specialist responsibility within a clearer regulated framework.

Does this replace registered builders?

No. Builders remain responsible for new homes, structural work, major extensions, and larger residential projects.

Why is this needed now?

Because the economic reality of modern renovations has changed while the threshold framework has not kept pace.
