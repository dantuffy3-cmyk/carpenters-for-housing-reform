The Problem

Victoria's domestic building threshold of around $10,000 no longer reflects the real cost of modern renovation projects.

  • Bathroom renovations commonly $20k–$40k
  • Kitchens often $25k–$70k
  • Decks and outdoor projects frequently exceed $15k

Many of these projects are coordinated by experienced carpenters on site, yet the current licensing framework does not clearly recognise that role.

The Proposed Reform

Introduce a Registered Residential Carpenter pathway recognising experienced carpenters with significant renovation experience.

This pathway would apply to defined renovation projects while maintaining the central role of licensed builders in major construction and new homes.

Experience Requirements

  • Completed carpentry apprenticeship
  • 10+ years industry experience
  • Demonstrated renovation project management
  • Coordination of licensed trades

Benefits for Victoria

  • More renovation capacity
  • Reduced industry bottlenecks
  • Better recognition of trade experience
  • Improved housing delivery

Other States

Several Australian states operate trade contractor licensing systems allowing qualified trades to contract work within defined scopes.

Exploring a similar renovation pathway in Victoria could recognise industry experience while maintaining strong standards.

Built from real renovation experience
