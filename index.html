Carpenters for Housing Reform Victoria

A trade-led, Victoria-first proposal to close the licence gap, unlock experienced carpenters for defined residential work, and help increase housing delivery — while keeping standards and consumer protections strong.

The problem

Builders today carry major responsibilities: council processes, contracts, insurance, compliance and project risk. Those obligations are essential — but they can slow delivery and bottleneck the trades behind them.

Victoria needs more practical capacity — without lowering standards.

The solution

Introduce a Registered Residential Carpenter pathway in Victoria. Builders remain responsible for new homes and major construction. Experienced carpenters gain defined responsibility for smaller works.

Key boundary

Builders lead new homes (site or factory) and extensions. Registered Residential Carpenters lead renovations and small secondary dwellings up to 30 m².

Proposed division of responsibility

Builders remain responsible for:

  • New homes (site-built or factory-built)
  • Extensions to existing homes
  • Multi-storey builds, complex structural projects
  • Residential developments

Registered Residential Carpenters could lead:

  • Renovations (bathrooms, laundries, general renovations)
  • Upgrades (windows/doors, internal alterations, structural carpentry modifications)
  • Decks/pergolas/verandas (where applicable)
  • Small granny flats / studios up to 30 m² (single storey)

All work remains subject to building approvals where required. Plumbing/electrical by licensed trades. Engineering sign-off where required. Clear scope and limits to reduce risk.

Safety and consumer protection

This reform is not deregulation. It aims to maintain contracts, compliance, and consumer protections — while recognising proven trade experience.

  • Defined scope, limits, and accountability
  • Bridging training in compliance / contracts / coordination
  • Appropriate insurance requirements
  • Auditability and standards enforcement
