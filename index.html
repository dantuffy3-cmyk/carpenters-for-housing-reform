Carpenters for Housing Reform Victoria

A practical, bounded reform proposal for renovation carpentry in Victoria.

This initiative proposes a Registered Residential Carpenter pathway for defined renovation work in existing homes, aimed at improving regulatory fit, recognising substantial on-site experience, and supporting renovation capacity while fully protecting domestic builder scope.

This proposal does not replace or dilute domestic builder scope. It proposes a tightly bounded pathway for defined renovation carpentry work in existing homes, with explicit exclusions for new home construction, major structural work, broader project responsibility, and other builder-led functions.

At a glance

What this pathway is — and is not.

What it is intended to cover

  • Defined renovation carpentry work in existing homes.
  • Bounded practical work carried out by experienced on-site carpenters.
  • A fit-for-purpose recognition pathway with clear limits and exclusions.
  • Improved regulatory fit for a class of work already occurring in the market.

What it is not intended to cover

  • New home construction.
  • Major structural work or major extensions.
  • Broader builder-level project control and responsibility.
  • Any open-ended builder authority by another name.

The problem

The current licensing framework does not always fit the practical reality of renovation work.

A significant share of residential renovation activity in Victoria occurs in existing homes and involves practical, carpentry-led work that differs from new home construction and from larger builder-led projects. In many cases, the current framework does not provide a clean, proportionate recognition pathway for experienced practitioners working within that defined renovation setting.

The result is a regulatory mismatch. Real work is being carried out in practice, but the licensing structure does not always reflect the character, limits, and practical reality of that work clearly enough.

The proposed solution

A Registered Residential Carpenter pathway for defined renovation work in existing homes.

The proposed Registered Residential Carpenter pathway is intended to improve regulatory fit by recognising a defined class of renovation carpentry work within clear boundaries. The aim is not to lower standards or create a parallel builder class. The aim is to better align recognition, accountability, and scope with the actual work being performed.

The proposal is focused on existing-home renovation, explicit exclusions, fit-for-purpose competency recognition, and a clearer distinction between bounded renovation carpentry work and the broader responsibilities that remain with domestic builders.

Core reform position

The argument is simple and defensible.

Recognise real renovation practice

A licensing framework should better reflect the practical work carried out by highly experienced carpenters in existing-home renovation settings.

Keep the pathway bounded

Any RRC model should be limited, clearly defined, and supported by explicit exclusions, competency requirements, and compliance settings.

Protect builder responsibility

Domestic builder scope should remain intact for new homes, major structural work, broader project responsibility, and other builder-led functions.

Why this matters

Better regulatory fit can support renovation capacity and housing opportunity.

Renovation work in existing homes forms a substantial part of Victoria’s housing activity. A clearer and more appropriate pathway for defined renovation carpentry work could improve industry clarity, support workforce recognition, and help the system better match the way smaller residential renovation projects are actually delivered.

Done properly, this can support consumers, practitioners, and housing outcomes without weakening standards or reducing the role of registered builders.

Leadership

Why this initiative is being put forward.

This initiative has been put forward by Daniel Sonsie of Sonsie Carpentry Pty Ltd as a practical reform proposal shaped by real renovation work, on-site experience, and concern about the mismatch between existing licensing settings and the way defined renovation activity is carried out in practice.

The purpose is not to weaken standards or displace builders. The purpose is to encourage serious consultation on a more proportionate and better-defined pathway for renovation carpentry in Victoria.
