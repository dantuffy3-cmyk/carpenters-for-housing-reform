Carpenters for Housing Reform Victoria

An industry-led initiative exploring a renovation-focused pathway for experienced carpenters to lead defined residential work — while builders remain responsible for new homes, new-home framing, extensions, and major construction.

Our Goal

Our goal is to explore a Registered Residential Carpenter pathway that supports builders, recognises experienced carpenters, and contributes to improving housing delivery in Victoria.

The focus is on defined renovation and carpentry works within clear limits, while builders remain responsible for new homes, framing, extensions, and major construction.

Victorian Trades Supporting Reform

Why This Reform Is Practical

Builders remain responsible for new homes, framing, extensions and major structural construction.

The proposed Registered Residential Carpenter pathway focuses specifically on renovation and carpentry works such as bathrooms, kitchens, internal carpentry, window replacements, decks, and small secondary dwellings.

This structure supports builders while recognising experienced carpenters who already perform much of this work across Victoria.

Housing Impact

Victoria faces increasing pressure to deliver housing while maintaining strong construction standards.

By recognising experienced carpenters through a clearly defined renovation-focused pathway, smaller residential projects may be delivered more efficiently while maintaining regulatory oversight and consumer protection.

Important Clarification

This initiative is not intended to replace licensed builders or alter the responsibilities of builders for new homes, structural construction, or multi-storey developments.

The focus is strictly on exploring a renovation-focused pathway for experienced carpenters within clearly defined limits.

The problem

Victoria needs more housing delivery capacity. Builders carry essential responsibilities (contracts, insurance, approvals and compliance), which can slow smaller projects and bottleneck the trades behind them.

The solution

Explore a renovation-focused Registered Residential Carpenter pathway with clear scope, limits and accountability — supporting builders and improving delivery speed while maintaining standards and consumer protections.

Proposed division of responsibility

Clear boundary (important)

This pathway is renovation-focused. It is not intended to cover new-home framing or new home construction.

Builders remain responsible for:

  • All new homes (site-built or factory-built)
  • All new-home framing and structural builds
  • Extensions and major structural alterations
  • Multi-storey builds, complex projects, residential developments

Registered Residential Carpenters could lead (renovation-focused):

  • Bathrooms, laundries, kitchens (renovation coordination)
  • Window and door upgrades / replacement
  • Internal fit-off carpentry (doors, trim, hardware, finish carpentry)
  • Decks / pergolas / verandas (where applicable)
  • Small granny flats / studios up to 30 m² (single storey)
  • Repair / rectification carpentry works within defined limits

Plumbing/electrical by licensed trades. Engineering sign-off where required. Approvals obtained where required. Clear scope and limits to manage risk and protect consumers.
